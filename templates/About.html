Preditions of Aflatoxin in Crops

Home About Prediction models Contacts

Aflatoxin is one of the most common pollutants in grains and cereals, causing serious health risks to consumers and financial losses to producers. Predicting whether aflatoxin will exceed certain tolerable limit or not given weather, soil properties and agricultural practice is important in decision making. In this paper, two machine learning algorithms: Gaussian Process Classification (GPC) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural networks have been used to predict aflatoxin contamination in maize and groundnuts. The performance of each approach (GPC and LSTM) was at least 85% showing great potential in classifying aflatoxin contamination. GPC with accuracy of 92% outperforms LSTM with accuracy of 85%. Findings from this study show that humidity and rainfall have great influence in predicting aflatoxin contamination compared to temperature and soil pH. This study presents a notable step towards predicting aflatoxin in crops using GPC and LSTM.

The aflatoxin contamination data for maize and groundnuts grown in 2017/2018 were obtained from Bahi, Kilosa, Kongwa, Meru and Masasi districts in Tanzania. For classification setting, we classified contamination data whether they exceed the legal limit of $10\mu g/kg$ or not. The two classes were above (label 1) or below (label 0). To match with weather data, rainfall, temperature and humidity data were obtained from Tanzania Meteorological Agency (TMA) on the same growing season of 2017/2018. The weather data and soil pH were used as independent variable $\left(\textbf{x}\in\mathbb{R}^{4}\right)$ for predicting the probability of aflatoxin contamination class label $\left(y_i\in\left(0,1\right)\right)$ exceeding the given limit of $10 \mu g/kg$. Data were divided into training data comprising 80\% and the remaining 20\% were used for testing.
